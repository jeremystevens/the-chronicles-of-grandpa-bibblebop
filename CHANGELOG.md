# Changelog

## v0.7.0 — Illustration Style
- Standardized illustration prompts.
- Established the Victorian fantasy engraving aesthetic.
- Removed captions, narration boxes, speech bubbles, and character labels from illustrations.
- Limited illustrations to natural environmental signage only.
- Focused each illustration on one memorable comedic scene.

## v0.6.0 — World Building
- Expanded the kingdom with recurring locations including the Royal Flat-Pack Furniture Emporium, Royal Department of Motor Vehicles, Royal Laundry, Royal Library, Village Market, and The Splintered Tooth Tavern.
- Began establishing recurring townspeople through brief interactions.
- Replaced real-world brands with original Bibblebop locations.

## v0.5.0 — Storytelling Philosophy
- Established the official Bibblebop writing philosophy.
- Episodes now resolve or transition from the previous cliffhanger.
- Adventures begin from varied locations instead of always Grandpa's cottage.
- Shifted Grandpa's tea gag toward the end of episodes.
- Reduced exposition and preserved the short Mad Lib pacing.
- Reinforced dead-serious narration of absurd events.

## v0.4.0 — Episodes I–VI
- Wrote Episodes I through VI.
- Added matching illustration prompts and artwork.
- Introduced recurring artifacts, villains, creatures, prophecies, and cliffhangers.

## v0.3.0 — Book Structure
- Created BOOK.md.
- Adopted short serialized episodes.
- Added a Foreword.
- Established one illustration per episode.

## v0.2.0 — Story Foundation
- Defined the Mad Lib driven storytelling format.
- Created Grandpa Bibblebop, Professor Bartholomew Vance, and the Royal Messenger.
- Began building the shared fantasy world.
