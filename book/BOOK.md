# Foreword

For centuries, the Royal Historians insisted these events never happened.

Unfortunately for them, Professor Bartholomew Vance kept remarkably detailed notes.

What follows is the complete and official record of the adventures of Grandpa Bibblebop—hero, reluctant legend, and a man who has spent an astonishing amount of his retirement simply trying to finish one peaceful cup of tea.

The Royal Archives accept no responsibility for dragons suffering emotional crises, magical household objects, time travel, Broadway-style tap dancing, or any prophecies discovered after the publication of this volume.

Should another catastrophe occur... this record will be updated accordingly.

# Episode I

## 🐉 The Dragon of Bumblebutt Junction

<br>

![Episode I — The Dragon of Bumblebutt Junction](/assets/episode1.png)

</div>

<br>

Long ago, in the glowing kingdom of Bumblebutt Junction, a fearsome dragon awakened after a thousand years of uninterrupted napping.

The ancient prophecy declared that only the legendary hero Grandpa Bibblebop could stop the beast.

Grandpa Bibblebop reached into the Sacred Chest and drew forth the enchanted Sword of Aggressive Compliments, a magical weapon said to have been forged by eccentric wizards during the Age of Extremely Questionable Decisions.

Clutching the weapon high above his head, he began to flop-sweat across the battlefield, confusing both allies and enemies alike.

The dragon paused, blinked twice, and muttered,

"I... wasn't prepared for this."

Grandpa pointed the sword toward the bewildered dragon.

"Your scales are absolutely magnificent."

The dragon blinked again.

"...Thank you?"

The villagers erupted into cheers as the dragon quietly admitted it had never been complimented before and flew away to "think about some things."

Grandpa smiled.

"Now perhaps I can finally finish my tea."

Just as he reached for his cup...

KNOCK. KNOCK. KNOCK.

Grandpa closed his eyes.

"...Come in."

The Royal Messenger slowly opened the front door.

"Grandpa..."

"Yes?"

"You're really going to hate this one."

# Episode II

## 🛁 The Public Bath House Incident

<br>

![Episode II — The Public Bath House Incident](/assets/episode2.png)

</div>

<br>

Following the dragon's surprisingly emotional departure, Grandpa Bibblebop had barely enough time to pour himself another cup of tea before the Royal Messenger dragged him back to the castle.

"There has been... another incident," the messenger explained.

Grandpa sighed.

"I haven't even finished my first cup."

This time, the trouble was centered around the kingdom's oldest public bath house.

Inside, the notorious villain Dr. Malice von Butter-Fingers had summoned the legendary garbage elemental known only as Garbagius Obnoxius.

Witnesses reported that the creature was behaving... unusually horny.

Professor Bartholomew Vance quickly adjusted his spectacles.

"I should clarify," he whispered. "The ancient texts fail to specify which definition of the word they intended."

"No need," Grandpa replied. "Let's just stop it."

Grandpa reached into his satchel and pulled out the only weapon he could find.

A half-eaten corn dog.

The room fell silent.

Dr. Malice von Butter-Fingers burst into uncontrollable laughter.

"You intend to defeat my greatest creation... with lunch?"

Grandpa shrugged.

"I skipped breakfast."

Without another word, he hurled the corn dog through the air.

It struck Garbagius Obnoxius squarely on the nose.

The creature stopped.

Sniffed.

Then began giggling uncontrollably.

Nobody knew why.

Not even Professor Vance.

Taking advantage of the confusion, Grandpa calmly walked over and closed the magical portal from which the creature had emerged.

Dr. Malice von Butter-Fingers threw his hands into the air.

"Barnacles and butter-fingers!"

Before anyone could stop him, he escaped through a cloud of suspiciously purple smoke, leaving behind exactly 420 confused rubber ducks.

Grandpa brushed the dust from his sleeves.

"Now..."

he said.

"...can I finally finish my tea?"

Professor Vance slowly unfolded yet another ancient scroll.

Grandpa looked at him.

"...Don't."

"I'm afraid I must."

"What is it this time?"

Professor Vance swallowed hard.

"It mentions something called..."

"Polymorph: Aggressive Goose."

Grandpa closed his eyes.

"...You're really going to hate this one."

# Episode III

## 🛋️ The Curse of the Chrono-Couch of Eternal Sloth

<br>

![Episode III — The Curse of the Chrono-Couch of Eternal Sloth](/assets/episode3.png)

</div>

<br>

Grandpa Bibblebop reluctantly followed Professor Bartholomew Vance to the Royal Library, where an ancient scroll described The Prophecy of the Flatulent Eclipse.

According to the prophecy, only the legendary Chrono-Couch of Eternal Sloth could prevent the kingdom from falling into complete and utter laziness.

Unfortunately, someone else had found it first.

"The Great Lord of the Printer Jam," Professor Vance whispered.

Grandpa frowned.

"I was hoping you'd say dragon."

The professor slowly shook his head.

"The Great Lord of the Printer Jam is an ancient, multi-dimensional cosmic entity fueled entirely by human frustration, broken office equipment, and flashing red error lights."

"...That somehow sounds worse."

The only clue pointed toward The Splintered Tooth, the rowdiest tavern in the kingdom and home to the hardest bread ever baked.

The innkeeper slid a frosty Mayonnaise Margarita across the table.

"It tastes terrible," he warned.

"But it'll cure just about any curse."

Grandpa took one sip.

"...He's right about the first part."

Before they could leave, the tavern doors burst open.

Standing outside was the magnificent Sourdough Phoenix, a mythical bird made entirely of golden-brown bread crust.

Instead of breathing fire, it blasted the street with clouds of delicious steam.

Within seconds, every adventurer nearby forgot the mission and sprinted toward the nearest bakery.

Grandpa reached for his shield.

Unfortunately, he had brought The Shield of Extreme Fragility.

The enormous enchanted shield looked indestructible...

Right until a single raindrop landed on it.

CRACK!

The shield exploded into thousands of tiny pieces.

Grandpa stared at the handle still clutched in his hand.

"I really should start reading the warranty."

Professor Vance unfolded another prophecy.

Grandpa groaned.

"Please tell me that's good news."

"I'm afraid not."

"What now?"

Professor Vance adjusted his spectacles.

"It says the Printer Jam was only... the warm-up."

Grandpa sighed.

"...Put the kettle on."

# Episode IV

## 🪈 The Flute of Mandatory Tap-Dancing

<br>

![Episode IV — The Flute of Mandatory Tap-Dancing](/assets/episode4.png)

</div>

<br>

Grandpa Bibblebop had just poured himself another cup of tea when the Royal Messenger burst through the front door.

"...You're really going to hate this one."

"I was afraid you'd say that."

Professor Bartholomew Vance arrived moments later carrying an ancient scroll.

"It appears the kingdom has become... crusty-bumfuzzled."

"Is that even a real word?" asked Grandpa.

"The prophecy seems to think so."

According to the scroll, only The Flute of Mandatory Tap-Dancing could stop The Blacksmith of Broken Hinges, a rogue craftsman who had secretly loosened exactly 341,892 hinges throughout the kingdom.

Already, castle doors were falling off.

Drawbridges leaned sideways.

Several toilet lids had become increasingly untrustworthy.

"The prophecy says the flute is hidden inside..."

Professor Vance adjusted his spectacles.

"...a 1990s Blockbuster Video Store."

Grandpa stared at him.

"I don't know what any of those words mean."

"Oh," said Professor Vance. "We'll need the Royal Chrono-Cart."

From behind a nearby tapestry, the professor wheeled out an ancient bronze machine covered in gears, clocks, and entirely unnecessary levers.

"I built it during my doctoral research."

"I thought your doctorate was in Ancient Draconic Archaeology."

"I have three."

Grandpa sighed.

"Of course you do."

Professor Vance pulled the largest lever.

The machine whistled...

Rattled...

Made several deeply concerning noises...

...and with a brilliant flash of blue light...

They were suddenly standing inside a 1990s Blockbuster Video Store.

Grandpa slowly looked around at endless shelves of VHS tapes.

"I miss dragons."

Before they could investigate further, they were ambushed by The Whispering Gunk-Toad, who immediately began whispering outrageous gossip.

"The Queen secretly lost an arm-wrestling match to a decorative turnip..."

Grandpa covered his ears.

"I don't think that's true."

"No," said Professor Vance.

"...but now I'm curious."

The Blacksmith of Broken Hinges stepped out from behind the Action Movies section.

"By the unseasoned boiled cabbage of my ancestors!" he shouted.

Grandpa instinctively cast Conjure Defensive Salad Tongs.

A gigantic glowing pair of wooden salad tongs floated into the air...

...gently picked up the villain...

...lifted him exactly three inches off the floor...

...and held him there.

The Blacksmith sighed.

"I was hoping for something more dramatic."

Professor Vance triumphantly handed Grandpa The Flute of Mandatory Tap-Dancing.

Grandpa played exactly one note.

Instantly...

Everyone within one hundred feet erupted into flawless, highly aggressive Broadway-style tap dancing.

The Blacksmith.

Professor Vance.

The Whispering Gunk-Toad.

Even a confused teenage Blockbuster employee who had absolutely no idea why medieval people kept appearing in his store.

Somewhere in the confusion, Grandpa accidentally grabbed a wooden salad tongs instead of the flute.

"...Well," he said.

"That explains nothing."

Professor Vance yanked the emergency return lever on the Royal Chrono-Cart.

The Blockbuster disappeared in a flash of blue light.

Seconds later they were back in the kingdom...

...although the castle clock now insisted it was Tuesday.

Grandpa decided not to ask.

He finally returned home and sat down with what was surely his sixteenth attempt at drinking a single cup of tea.

Professor Vance slowly unfolded yet another ancient prophecy.

Grandpa didn't even look up.

"...What now?"

The professor swallowed nervously.

"It says..."

*'Beware the cathedral gargoyle made entirely of 400 pounds of creamery butter... especially during unusually warm weather.'*

Grandpa stared silently into his teacup.

For the first time in years...

He was completely flabberghasted.

Just then, somewhere deep inside the Royal Palace...

The Royal Palace Guard's Under-Armor Chainmail Jockstrap became sentient.

"...Oh dear," said Grandpa.

# Episode V

## 🎩 The Top Hat of Aggressive Pigeon Summoning

<br>

![Episode V — The Top Hat of Aggressive Pigeon Summoning](/assets/episode5.png)

</div>

<br>

Grandpa Bibblebop had almost finished his morning tea when the Royal Messenger burst through the front door.

"...You're really going to hate this one."

Grandpa slowly set down his cup.

"I was beginning to miss those words."

Professor Bartholomew Vance hurried in carrying another ancient prophecy.

"The kingdom has become... decidedly anserine."

Grandpa sighed.

"That usually means geese."

"Worse."

Professor Vance unrolled the scroll.

"Lord Gluteus, The Yeast Overlord, has begun covering the countryside in enchanted sourdough starter."

Already, entire villages were slowly disappearing beneath rising waves of bubbling bread dough.

"The prophecy says only The Top Hat of Aggressive Pigeon Summoning can stop him."

Grandpa blinked.

"I don't suppose it's nearby."

Professor Vance shook his head.

"It is currently being held at..."

He adjusted his spectacles.

"...the Royal Department of Motor Vehicles."

Grandpa stared at him.

"We have one of those?"

"We do now."

Moments later, the Royal Chrono-Cart deposited them inside a brightly lit waiting room filled with knights holding numbered tickets.

A dragon argued with a clerk over a failed smog inspection.

A peasant attempted to pay his registration fee with two chickens.

A sign on the wall read:

NOW SERVING: B-14

Professor Vance looked down at their ticket.

F-84,317

"...Oh."

Fortunately, Professor Vance remembered the Royal Wizard Discount Window.

Five minutes later, Grandpa emerged wearing The Top Hat of Aggressive Pigeon Summoning.

"It doesn't seem so ba—"

WHOOSH!

Grandpa rocketed into the air...

...followed immediately by forty extremely confused pigeons that materialized inside his clothes.

"WHY ARE THERE PIGEONS IN MY PANTS?!"

Professor Vance consulted the instruction manual.

"Ah."

"What?!"

"It says that's normal."

Lord Gluteus suddenly burst through the bakery section wearing stale pumpernickel armor and wielding The Baguette of Absolute Doom.

"BY THE GLOSS OF MY MOTHER'S LINOLEUM!"

he roared.

The battle had begun.

Grandpa immediately cast Summon Aggressive Jazz Hands.

Everyone within thirty feet dropped what they were holding and launched into perfectly synchronized Broadway jazz hands.

At the same moment, deafening swing music erupted from Grandpa's mouth.

Blinding stage lights blasted from his eyes.

A phantom announcer bellowed,

"AND NOW... A SOLO!"

The Glitter-Back Sloth-Goose landed on Grandpa's shoulders.

"HONK!"

It pointed one wing toward the nearest puddle.

"I'm a little busy!" Grandpa shouted.

The bird honked louder.

In the confusion, Lord Gluteus slipped on a half-empty bottle of generic ranch dressing that nobody remembered bringing.

He crashed face-first into...

...a block of salted butter.

Instantly, the Yeast Overlord burst into joyful sobbing.

He began dancing.

The Baguette of Absolute Doom flew harmlessly across the room.

Professor Vance smiled.

"I suspected that might happen."

Grandpa finally caught his breath.

"I'd really like to meet whoever writes these prophecies."

Professor Vance slowly unfolded another scroll.

Grandpa groaned.

"...Don't."

"I'm afraid I must."

"It says..."

*'Beware the unsolicited tactical advice of the Enchanted Avocado.'*

Grandpa became completely discombobulator-ed.

His left eyebrow quietly migrated toward his chin.

He accidentally lifted his left slipper...

...and took a sip of tea from it.

Just then...

Somewhere deep inside the Royal Castle...

A public toilet brush opened its eyes for the very first time.

"...Oh dear," whispered Grandpa.
