# WRITING_GUIDELINES.md

# The Chronicles of Grandpa Bibblebop
## Official Writing Guidelines

This document defines the creative direction of the series. Whenever writing a new episode, these guidelines take precedence over adding complexity or unnecessary lore.

---

# Core Mission

The goal of The Chronicles of Grandpa Bibblebop is to make readers genuinely laugh out loud.

Every episode should aim to surprise the reader with escalating absurdity while treating every ridiculous event with complete seriousness.

If forced to choose between:
- Better worldbuilding
- A bigger laugh

Choose the laugh.

---

# The Golden Rules

## 1. Mad Lib First

Every episode begins with a completed Mad Lib.

Then:
- Fix the grammar.
- Add only enough connecting sentences for smooth reading.
- Add one or more running jokes naturally.
- End with one cliffhanger.
- Stop.

No padding.

---

## 2. Every Episode Must Connect

Every episode must acknowledge the previous episode.

Two acceptable approaches:

### Continue the cliffhanger
The previous cliffhanger becomes today's adventure.

### Transition from the cliffhanger
Resolve the previous cliffhanger in a few funny sentences, then launch the new adventure.

Never ignore a previous cliffhanger.

---

## 3. Get to the Humor Quickly

The reader should encounter absurdity almost immediately.

Avoid lengthy introductions, exposition, or backstory.

---

## 4. Keep Episodes Short

Episodes should read like serialized comic strips or Saturday morning adventure serials.

Fast pacing.
Short paragraphs.
Minimal exposition.

---

## 5. Deadpan Delivery

Nobody questions the absurdity.

The world treats impossible events as completely ordinary.

---

# Episode Formula

1. Resolve or transition from the previous cliffhanger.
2. Show Grandpa living his ordinary life.
3. Introduce today's ridiculous problem.
4. Let the Mad Lib drive the adventure.
5. Include recurring jokes naturally.
6. End with a new cliffhanger.
7. Grandpa almost enjoys a peaceful cup of tea... but doesn't.

---

# Comedy Philosophy

Humor comes from surprise, not randomness.

Typical rhythm:

Normal.

Normal.

Normal.

Absolute insanity.

Deadpan reaction.

Move on.

Every episode should contain at least one unforgettable laugh-out-loud moment.

Never explain the joke.

---

# Grandpa Bibblebop

- Reluctant hero.
- Wants a quiet retirement.
- Deadpan personality.
- Constantly interrupted before enjoying peace.

---

# Professor Bartholomew Vance

- Scholar of impossible things.
- Loves prophecies.
- Invents bizarre devices.
- Treats nonsense as established science.

---

# The Royal Messenger

Use occasionally—not every episode.

Always arrives at the worst possible time.

---

# Worldbuilding

The kingdom should feel alive.

Grandpa can begin episodes anywhere:

- Grandpa's Cottage
- Village Market
- Bakery
- Blacksmith
- General Store
- Village Well
- The Splintered Tooth Tavern
- Town Square
- Royal Castle
- Royal Library
- Royal Laundry
- Royal Department of Motor Vehicles
- Royal Flat-Pack Furniture Emporium
- Farms
- Orchard
- Forest
- Ancient Ruins
- Bridge
- Windmill
- River Crossing

Reuse locations naturally.

Recurring townspeople should have brief interactions only.

No lengthy backstories.

---

# Running Gags

Examples include:

- Grandpa never finishing his tea.
- Professor Vance's absurd prophecies.
- Returning villains and magical artifacts.
- Tiny callbacks to previous episodes.
- The world remembering previous adventures.

---

# Illustration Style

Every illustration should:

- Depict one memorable scene.
- Treat absurdity seriously.
- Use black-and-white pen-and-ink.
- Heavy cross-hatching.
- Arthur Rackham / Gustave Doré inspiration.
- Victorian fantasy engraving style.
- Portrait orientation.
- No cartoon style.
- No captions.
- No narration boxes.
- No speech bubbles.
- No character labels.
- Only natural environmental signage.

---

# Final Philosophy

Build a world readers love returning to.

Don't write a long fantasy novel.

Write one hilarious episode at a time.

If each episode makes someone laugh out loud, the series is succeeding.
